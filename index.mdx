<div className="mt-16 mb-16 max-w-5xl mx-auto px-6">
  <h2 className="text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 dark:text-white mb-4">
    Quick Start
  </h2>
  <p className="text-lg text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-400 mb-8">
    Get up and running with Netcatty in minutes
  </p>

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Download and Install">
      Download the latest release for your platform from [GitHub Releases](https://github.com/binaricat/Netcatty/releases/latest).
      
      Available for macOS (Universal), Windows (x64/arm64), and Linux (x64/arm64).

      <Note>
        **macOS users**: After downloading, remove the quarantine attribute:
        ```bash
        xattr -cr /Applications/Netcatty.app
        ```
      </Note>
    </Step>

    <Step title="Add Your First Host">
      Launch Netcatty and click the **+** button in the Vault to add a new host.
      
      Configure your connection:
      - **Hostname**: Your server's IP or domain
      - **Port**: SSH port (default 22)
      - **Username**: Your SSH username
      - **Authentication**: Choose password or SSH key

      Click **Save** to add the host to your vault.
    </Step>

    <Step title="Connect and Explore">
      Double-click your host to open a terminal session. Try these features:
      
      - **Split panes**: Right-click the terminal tab → Split Horizontal/Vertical
      - **SFTP**: Click the folder icon to browse remote files
      - **Port forwarding**: Access the Port Forwarding panel to create tunnels
      
      Your workspace state is automatically saved and restored on next launch.
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</div>
